What is Business Analytics?

Business Analytics is the use of:

  • Data from various sources
  • Information technology tools and platforms
  • Statistical analysis methods
  • Quantitative methods and techniques
  • Mathematical or computer-based models

To help managers gain improved insight about their business operations and make better, fact-based decisions.

Key Benefits:

Reduced costs
Better risk management
Faster decisions
Better productivity
Enhanced profitability
Customer satisfaction

Data for Business Analytics

What is Data?

Numbers or textual data that are collected through some type of measurement process. Raw facts and figures that have not yet been processed.

What is Information?

The result of analyzing data; that is, extracting meaning from data to support evaluation and decision making. Data that has been processed into a meaningful form.

Data Quality Dimensions

Reliability

Data are accurate and consistent.

A tire pressure gauge that consistently reads several pounds of pressure below the true value is not reliable, although it is valid because it does measure tire pressure.

Validity

Data correctly measures what it is supposed to measure.

The number of calls to a customer service desk might be counted correctly (reliable) but not valid if used to assess customer dissatisfaction, as many calls may be simple queries.

Big Data

Big data refers to massive amounts of business data (volume) from a wide variety of sources (variety), much of which is available in real time (velocity), and much of which is uncertain or unpredictable (veracity).

Applications of Business Analytics

Marketing & Sales

Pricing

Setting prices for consumer and industrial goods, government contracts, and maintenance contracts

Customer Segmentation

Identifying and targeting key customer groups in retail, insurance, and credit card industries

Merchandising

Determining brands to buy, quantities, and allocations

Location

Finding the best location for bank branches and ATMs, or where to service industrial equipment

Operations & Management

Supply Chain Design

Determining the best sourcing and transportation options and finding the best delivery routes

Staffing

Ensuring appropriate staffing levels and capabilities, and hiring the right people

Health Care

Scheduling operating rooms, improving patient flow, purchasing supplies, and predicting health risk factors
